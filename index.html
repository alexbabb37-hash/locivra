Why Locivra

Security teams have more locations than attention.

When budgets and analyst time are limited, the first decision is not what intervention to deploy. It is where a closer review could create the most value.

?

The current challenge

Location comparisons often depend on scattered data, spreadsheets, recent incidents or inconsistent judgment. Teams spend time building context before analysis can begin.

→

A defensible starting point

Locivra creates a consistent first-pass priority list, shows the historical crime factors influencing each score and produces an output stakeholders can review.

How it works

From an address list to an explainable priority queue.

The workflow is intentionally simple so users can reach the decision faster without treating the score as an automated verdict.

1

Share 5–20 locations

Provide Toronto addresses in a spreadsheet or CSV and confirm the decision the portfolio review should support.

2

Compare historical exposure

Locivra applies the selected radius, distance weighting, crime-category weights and Toronto comparison context.

3

Review the output

Receive a ranked portfolio, plain-language score explanation, PDF report and options for deeper follow-up on priority locations.

Data and methodology

Transparent by design—not a black box.

Version 1.0 is a prototype methodology informed by environmental criminology, place-based research, CPTED and RTM principles. Its assumptions are disclosed so they can be tested and improved.

Historical reported crime

Current analysis uses Toronto Police Service Open Data for Assault, Robbery, Break and Enter, Auto Theft, and Theft Over $5,000.

Distance-weighted exposure

Incidents closer to the selected location receive more influence than incidents near the edge of the analysis radius.

Prototype crime weights

Categories are weighted for their current estimated relevance to retail safety, staff and customer harm, and property loss.

Relative comparison

Location exposure is compared with broader Toronto patterns to create sub-scores and an overall 0–100 priority score.

Visible assumptions

Reports state the radius, data source, date coverage, contributors and limitations so the result can be interrogated.

Validation before expansion

Radius, decay logic, crime weights and future environmental layers require expert feedback and empirical testing.

Responsible-use boundary: Locivra uses historical reported-crime data to support location prioritization. It does not predict that crime will occur, certify a location as safe or unsafe, or replace internal incident data, professional judgment and site assessment.

Decision-ready output

Make the priority easier to review and explain.

Each assessment packages the portfolio view and the reasoning behind it into a shareable format.

  • Ranked portfolio of submitted locations
  • Overall and crime-category priority scores
  • Plain-language explanation of key contributors
  • Documented data scope, radius and limitations
  • Optional site-level follow-up for priority locations

Pilot program
